  • ACTION VERBS

    As the name suggests, an action verb is used to represent the action done by the subject in a sentence. Action verbs cannot be used as linking verbs. They have only one function, and that is to denote the action in a sentence.

  • MODAL VERBS

    A modal verb is a type of verb that contextually indicates a modality such as a likelihood, ability, permission, request, capacity, suggestion, order, obligation, or advice. Modal verbs generally accompany the base form of another verb having semantic content

  • LINKING VERBS

    A linking verb connects the subject of a sentence to an adjective, noun, or pronoun that completes the meaning of the verb. Common linking verbs are the forms of the verb to be; verbs relating to the five senses; and the verbs become, appear, and seem.
